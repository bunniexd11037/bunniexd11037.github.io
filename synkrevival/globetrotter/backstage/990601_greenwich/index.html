Behind The Scenes - 1 June 1999

Those of you who are signed up to the official Jamiroquai mailing list will have read last week that the band were going to be performing live to an estimated 10 million people in Japan via satellite for NHK (Japan Broadcasting Corporation).

Well, we didn't want the fans to miss out on this event so we sent our roving reporter David Rowe with his camera to get the lowdown on what went on.

On the afternoon on Tuesday 1 June 1999 London was getting nearer to Summer with an amazingly warm Spring day. The sky was blue, there were no clouds in the sky, and what was the highlight - Jamiroquai on Japanese TV, being broadcast live from Greenwich Naval College in South East London.

This was a pretty big event with a large number of top music acts performing. It hasn't been widely publicised in the UK so it was an excellent opportunity to go and get some exclusive photos. The venue was full of people involved in the production and filming of it all as there were acts performing in various parts of the college grounds. Jamiroquai were today sharing the room with another big UK act - Culture Club.

I arrived at about 13:15 to catch the end of Culture Club. Jamiroquai were due on the stage for their soundcheck and rehearsal at 13:30 and for the live performance at just before 14:00.

There were about 25 people in the hall, mostly working on the broadcast so it was number 1 priority to stay clear of them and the vast array of wires that filled the floor.

Prior to the television performance Jay was hatless - showing off the ponytails in his hair. However, when the cameras were rolling, the hat was firmly on. The soundcheck consisted of a performance of Canned Heat, followed by the band practicing amongst themselves. As soon as the soundcheck finished Jay went off and left the rest of the band to chat. 10 minutes until showtime.

As the time ticked down the cameras moved closer and with just over a minute to go before they went live, there was no sign of Jay who was required for an interview prior to the performance. 30 seconds to go and still no sign of him so it was up to Toby and Derrick to step in to take his place. However, as soon as the cameras started rolling, Jay arrived and the interview went ahead as planned - this time with Toby and Derrick standing close by. Following a few questions and answers via satellite from the fans in Japan it was time to get down to business.

The band chose to perform Canned Heat (which entered the UK singles chart at number 4 two days previously), followed by Black Capricorn Day (from Synkronized), and then finished off with Cosmic Girl. The vibe was there (was I the only one singing?), the band looked good and they put on a super fine show. [ image: Jamiroquai performing ] [ image: DJ D-Zire ]

 

Chatting to Derrick and Toby afterwards, their new found fatherhood is fantastic and they can't wait to play some of the new tracks live. With the bands' pre-tour rehearsals drawing to a close, the whole band are now desperate to get on the road - starting on 9 June in Brighton, England.

Moving outside, Jay was cornered by a number of children looking for autographs to which he was happy to respond. Whilst all this was going on, I caught this shot of Toby and Darren (DJ D-Zire) joking around. [ image: Toby and Darren ]

As we all made our separate ways from the venue, I managed to convince Derrick that it was about time we saw a decent photo of him on the web site, and being one to oblige, this is what we came up with. [ image: Derrick McKenzie ]
