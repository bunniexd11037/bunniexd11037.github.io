Synkronized Tour - 9 June 1999

Warm spring weather greeted the south coast seaside town of Brighton, England on Wednesday 9th June 1999 where Jamiroquai were to kick off the first date of their Synkronized tour. Once again, the Official website was there to report from the concert to bring the information straight to you, the fans.

The Brighton Centre, located right by the sea, was bustling with people eager to see Jay and the rest of the gang do their thing - and also get to hear some of the tracks from the album which was set for release in the UK the following Monday.

Inside, the set was hidden by black curtains and, as the sound and lighting engineers took to their positions, the venue was full and the crowd ready to see Jamiroquai's first full live concert since December 1997 at Battersea Power Station in London.

With the set hidden, the crowd were unable to see when the band were to strike their first note. When they did at around 9pm, and the curtains opened, we knew what we'd been missing.

Launching straight into High Times, the band, including new bassist Nick Fyffe, were on top form. After rehearsing for the tour for well over a month - longer than ever before - one song blended into another for a nonstop trip through the past three albums with a couple of covers thrown in for good measure.

 

Jay spoke quite a few times, at one point telling the crowd that he wrote the ballad Falling for his girlfriend because "I love her loads".

Most of the tracks were taken from Synkronized, with ones from Travelling taking second place. As the concert took place prior to the release Synkronized, it took a while for the crowd to get into the new tracks.

Travelling Without Moving provided the cue for Wallis to enter the stage to dance and boogie around the rest of the musicians. Despite 18 months away from the tour circuit, plenty of Canned Heat has kept him in fine rhythm.

Virtual Insanity caused some laughter - half way through the first verse Jay couldn't continue as he thought he was singing the wrong verse (he wasn't !). After a bit of chat it turned out that nothing was wrong and when they started again, they pretty soon almost stopped - with Jay teasing Toby that it was his fault. This was certainly unrehearsed, as was confirmed by Toby after the show.

The crowd were noisy - very noisy - and they really went for Deeper Underground, the UK number 1 single the band released in the Summer of 1998. The track was accompanied by some excellent lighting effects. [ image: Simon Katz ] [ image: Nick Fyffe ] [ image: Jay Kay ]

Talking about the conflict in Kosovo, there was only one song to play - Too Young To Die. The crowd really seemed to enjoy this classic from the first album.

The concert came to a close at 11pm, 2 hours after they started, which was by far the longest concert I've ever seen them play.

Shortly after the show, Nick came out to sign a few autographs for the fans who had stayed inside the venue to congratulate him on him playing so well. After the show I caught up with Nick and asked if he was struck by nerves. He said that, surprisingly, he slept really well the night before and when he woke up, did some cleaning around the house. However, he got really nervous about an hour before the show but afterwards he was in such a good mood he chose to celebrate a successful first concert with family and friends and a bottle of champagne.

 

The evening then drew to a close and we all went home into the night. This was just the beginning of the tour for the band and after the set was packed away onboard their fleet of trucks, they set off for Manchester for the next date. This was an excellent concert and if they're coming near your town over the coming months, I highly recommend it.

Words and Photos: David Rowe (June 1999)
