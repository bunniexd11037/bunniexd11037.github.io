First of all, the mere fact that they went to Mexico City was absolutely exciting. I went to the press conference and meeting the band was very interesting, although Jay couldnt make it because of food poisoning on his flight (I ate some bad fish, man -he said on the concert). Toby didnt answer when he was asked something about the change of the bands style since the former bassist left. I asked myself what the bands three overall favorite songs were, but when everybody started stirring and discussing about it, Toby again said he would skip my question too (It would be too boring if everyone just said which songs are their favorites -he said). Although Derrick immediately answered Cosmic Girl when I asked. It was funny that they were asked whether they would finally be in one of the videos. As for the concert, being the really big fan I am, I was completely satisfied for the bands song selection. I had the doubt of whether they would just play the really famous songs from their last two albums, but they played Space Cowboy and Light Years, although they didnt play any from their first album, Emergency on Planet Earth. It was pretty exciting that they opened the concert with High Times. Really violent, compared to other tunes! Then they blended it with Alright and the auditorium went completely berserk. They played Cosmic Girl, Supersonic, Black Capricorn Day, Canned Heat, Travelling without Moving, Planet Home, and all the before mentioned songs. Their performance was really good, we heard an intro by the percussionist to one of their medleys, as well as superb sound quality. I was a bit skeptic about the new bass player being a bass player who admired (i.e. idolized) Jamiroquais former bass player. His performance was satifying, although in some songs I didnt notice him playing the full bass lines with arrangements and everything like Stuart did. Besides, the way Jay comforted him after he played the intro to Travelling without moving gave away a picture of good boy, you didnt mess up. This might be a bit harsh from me, but I believe that Jamiroquai wont find a bass player as good vibed and smooth playing as their former one. However, after seeing Jay light up the stage with a white, Indian chief-like hat and dance with that incredible air surfing thing he does and the whole auditorium going wild, I could hardly think of anything else except oh my god, theyre so good!
