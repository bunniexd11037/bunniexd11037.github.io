What can I say? The concert was worderful, and Im glad that I had a chance to attend a live concert of theirs!
I went with my friend and my dad, I was telling them that we should arrive there at 5:00 because it would be busy..... but, my dad didnt want to wait in line that long, so we arrived there at 6:00. Boy, were they suprised to see all the people standing in line!
When we arrived, the line was wrapped around the corner, so I was feeling kind of blue, but we did arrive just in time, because even more people came. We stood by the alley of the Riveria, and Wallice walked by us, and standing to the left of us on the street, was Simon.
Ok, so when we walked through the doors, I was so excited and nervous that I was shaking!
We went into the room where the concert was being held, I saw that there were alot of empty places in the front to stand. So I grabbed her arm and ran over there just in time!
There were two people in front of me, and they were taller, but I could still see the stage...oh, yeah! We were about two feet away from the speakers on the right side of the audience. So we could hear everything very well!
After waiting forever what seemed like forever (45 minutes) they opened with High Times. Let me tell you, it was all just fantastic! Everybody in the audience singing every word with Jay, it was beautiful!! Of course he was sweating from the dancing and singing and you could tell on his shirt, so he joked, And the prize for the wet t-shirt contest goes to....
About half-way through the performance, Jay spoke about the crisis in Kosovo, so they dedicated Too Young To Die to the victims. You could tell that some of the fans didnt know the song... only about half of the audience was singing along. But was very pretty.
The played Supersonic and Wallis on his didjeridoo was perfect! Since that was the only song he did, he danced around the stage to get the atmosphere in the audience even more exciting! He waved and smiled to people; he waved to me one time, so I waved back, and a little bit later I flashed him the peace sign, so he smiled and gave the peace sign back at me!
I cant exactly remember (I was just too happy at the time!) but I think the last song was Depper Underground When that was finished, Jay told us about how much he loved Chicago,and they are flattered by all the support over the years. Then he said that they would be back in November! (Im already begging my dad to take me again!!) Of course, they did an encore, and they played Canned Heat with everybody singing along and boogie-ing down to the end of the perfect night.

Last night was truly an amazing Jamiroquai concert. Chicago gave it up for the next Rolling Stones (35+ years) that will mesmerize world through its music.

For us lucky supporters that arrived at the Riv before 5:30 p.m., we were fortunate enough to see JK walking down the street with no bodyguard. He talked to us as if we were his buddies, signed everything people gave him; concert tickets, CDs, CD sleeves, pieces of paper, t-shirts, buffalo man posters people ripped off the street poles, and was posing like crazy for the people that had their cameras.

Jasons aura is so calming and uplifting. Hes just as normal as we who are not in the entertainment industry.

When the concert started, the sold out arena of 1500+ people went crazy. JK even acknowledge Chicago as his favorite place to play because we give it up. As the first notes of High Times kicked off Jamiroquais show in Chicago after a two and a half year absence, the audience sang every single word.

I, having the honor of being in the front hanging on the rail, could hear the audience singing and see the amazement on the bands faces as we partied with them. JK could have put the mic down and we would have sang all night for him.

After an amazing rendition of Cosmic Girl people were screaming and thats when JK came over to me and I jumped on the rail to tell him to play Falling he grabbed my hand so I wouldnt fall and he could hear what I was saying. His response, We didnt rehearse that one. I thought it was the funniest thing because I was expecting the Wembley song list. But, he led the band into another hit song, Planet Home.

The concert was all dance. It was funk and jazz together. The horn section was too funky! JKs dance moves were smooth as well as Wallis whos Didgeridoo performance on Supersonic nearly brought the house down. That should most definitely be the next release after Black Capicorn Day. And speaking of BCD, my favorite song on the new release, the audience almost went into cardiac arrest.

For those of you who saw the webcast, it was better if you were at the Riviera. For those of you who will see Jamiroqaui soon, youre in for the time of your lives. Im still singing and smiling. JK did say, they will be back in Chicago in November. Im there.

Remember, they may need some wings to get to the next gig so bring extra gifts you who partake in the natural foods of the earth.
