 Score : 10/10
Leave it to Jay Kay and the Boys to funk up the Dirty South with their special brand of funkadelic delicious groovin tunage. The scene at the Tabernacle was intensely rich with die-hard fans (i.e. own all the albums, know all the words to the songs), one-hit thrill seekers (i.e. virtual insanity), and curious virgins (first time Jamiroquai listeners). Young and old, black and white, male and female vibed together in perfect harmony as the Fab Brits took the stage and strapped us in for a one booty-shaking, back-braking, no-mistaking fellowship of divine sound. The energy exuded from Jay and the gang was non-stop from start to finish with the crowd being entranced by the lyrical invasion of mystical melodies and hyped-up head-bopping sounds from the band weve grown to know and love. This show was definitely one to put on the memory list of all-time favorite concerts. From the screams, yells, and buckets of sweat coming from the sold-out crowd at the Tabernacle, one can truly say Jamiroquai has made a definite impact on us Yankees. With people driving from Missouri, South Carolina, and Florida just to see our favs transpire their ethereal magic upon us, Jamis popularity is definitely growing by leaps and bounds. Hopefully with the release of their fourth album and their continued touring, the rest of the population of music lovers unhip to the majesty that we know as JAMIROQUAI, will get on board the Buffalo Man express and taste the filling feeling of the Space Cowboy and his posse.
